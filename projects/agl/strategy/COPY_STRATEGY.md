## Positioning

AGL is the movement-intelligence layer and honest guide for experienced advisors weighing a transition — reading the public record on ~725,000 U.S. registrations so the biggest decision of an advisor's career runs on evidence instead of recruiter pitches, and telling the truth even when the truth is "stay."

One-line compression (for meta descriptions, OG, podcast boilerplate):
> Every recruiter has a stake in your answer. We show you the whole board — and tell you the truth, even when it's "stay."

**Flags — the only lines the research says need attention:**

1. **[VERIFY] "A guide who has walked hundreds of advisors through this"** (Why-we-exist card 3). "Hundreds" is a quantified experience claim not in the proof inventory (INTAKE §5). This ICP fact-checks for a living; one soft number undermines the data positioning. Until Chris confirms the guide's real count, soften to *"A guide who has sat on your side of hundreds of these conversations"* → still [VERIFY], or the always-safe *"A guide who does nothing but this — and only when you ask."*
2. **"We've probably already found you."** (assessment section). Calculated risk: it flips the surveillance frame the ICP hates (name-on-every-list) into public-record honesty. The research says name-leak anxiety is the *default posture* (throwaway accounts). The mitigation is already in place ("The report is yours alone; no one is notified") — keep the line, but the privacy counter must never be separated from it on any future surface that reuses this copy. If it ever tests cold, fallback: *"Your record is already public. We just read it better."*
3. **Missing beat: "even if the answer is stay" arrives late.** The unmonetized-honesty gap (Competitive Gap #1) is AGL's only unclaimed ground, but on the homepage it first appears inside the who-we-are disclosure. Recommended micro-refinement (copy-only, fits existing layout): append to the assessment section's privacy line or the 7/3-min chip row one short clause — e.g. dek ends *"…no one is notified — and if the honest read is 'stay put,' that's the report you'll get."* This is the single highest-value copy addition available on the page.
4. **Guide name/photo placeholder** — still [VERIFY] per intake. If the guide is Lane: peer framing only ("an advisor who works with advisors in and after transition"), never "made the transition herself," never recruiter framing.

Everything else: ship as-is.

## Pain Section (order + framing — governs new surfaces; homepage already conforms)

1. **Every input is a sales pitch** — lead here because it's the ambient condition ("bombarded by recruiters") and it's the pain AGL's model uniquely answers. Language: "stake in the answer," "the whole board."
2. **Deciding blind / in secret** — asking openly risks tipping off the firm (Fidelity-custodian story, throwaway accounts). Language: "decide in private, on evidence."
3. **The math is rigged or inflated** — clawbacks, 7–8 year notes, grid recapture ("LPL more than recovers with their grid"). Language: plain ranges, labeled illustrative, "not a recruiter's rosy math."
4. **Will my clients follow / am I ready** — the soul-crushing fear ("Only 25% came with me"). Frame as an evidence question, not a courage question — that's what the assessment answers.

## Services Framing

Map every offering to the desire cluster, never to internal logic:
- Movement intelligence → **"See the whole board"** (peer-comparison desire; what r/CFP threads crowdsource badly, done properly from public filings).
- Assessment → **"Know where you stand — even if the answer is stay"** (private, self-serve, honest; the MQL).
- Private call / guide → **"A peer in the room, not a recruiter on the phone"** (Lane = peer, per §4; no pitch, no pipeline).
- The model AGL represents (when it comes up, incl. episode content) → sell the MODEL only: full ownership · high recurring fee-based revenue · robust back office so advisors can serve · camaraderie with successful peers. Axiom named only in disclosure language, never sold as entity.

## Proof That Moves This ICP

In priority order: (1) public-record footing — SEC/FINRA/BrokerCheck citations on every data claim; (2) real numbers in native units — bps, payout %, T-12, note terms; vague "great economics" reads as recruiter-speak; (3) disclosed conflict — the Axiom disclosure IS proof; (4) willingness to say "stay" — demonstrated, not claimed; (5) peer voice — Lane-as-peer, unvarnished specifics. Explicitly deferred: testimonials (Memphis footage [VERIFY: edits + compliance review]), "90+ advisors" [VERIFY with Jerry]. Never: invented stories, unlabeled ranges, "unbiased" without adjacent disclosure.

## Episode Page Strategy

Follows PAGE_SYSTEM §3 anatomy exactly (breadcrumb/kicker → H1 hook → dek → player → payload → mid-post CTA → end conversion block → related → footer). Strategy-level rules on top of the skeleton:

- **Mid-post CTA framing:** always the natural continuation of the insight just delivered, in assessment language — pattern: *"[This pattern] is one of the signals the readiness assessment reads against your own record. See where you stand — 7 questions, 3 minutes, results are yours alone."* Never a banner, never "subscribe."
- **End conversion block:** primary = assessment (MQL), secondary = "Book a private call" (SQL). Restate the two objection-handlers every time: private/no-one-notified + we'll-say-stay-if-it's-stay.
- **Numbers discipline:** every economic figure = labeled illustrative range from publicly reported transitions + "not an offer, estimate, or guarantee." Every movement figure = public SEC/FINRA/BrokerCheck footing. No invented advisor stories; real r/CFP sentiment may be paraphrased ("advisors describe…"), quoted verbatim only after re-verification against live threads (research sourcing note).
- **FAQPage JSON-LD** only from real Q/As in the body; question-shaped H2s are the AI-answer/snippet surface — write them as the literal questions advisors type.

### Episode Headline Formula (stakes-phrased, per PAGE_SYSTEM §3)

> **[The thing they typed, in native units] + [what it does to their money, clients, or next 24 months] — second person, zero episode numbers, zero topic labels.**

Templates:
1. *"What [trigger/term] actually means for [your book / your take-home / your next 24 months]"*
2. *"The [cost/clause/pattern] nobody prices in when [decision moment]"*
3. *"[Their question, verbatim] — the honest math / the pattern in the public record"*

Test: if the headline could caption a conference session ("Understanding Transition Packages"), it fails. If it could be the first line of an r/CFP post a veteran would upvote, it passes. Kill-list applies at full strength (no "opportunity," "maximize," "unlock," no urgency).

## Nav Labels

Current nav stays: **How we help · Intelligence · Assessment · Who we are · [Book a private call]**

**New hub label: "The Lab"** (URL stays `/resources/` for search clarity — label and slug need not match).

Why "The Lab" beats "Resources":
- "Resources" is the label on every recruiter/BD site this ICP is fleeing — AdvisorHub-style pattern-match to the noise. The kill list's spirit (no LinkedIn-consultant vocabulary) applies to nav too.
- "The Lab" is already the brand's noun (Advisor Growth **Lab**; newsletter "Inside the Lab"), signals daily ongoing output rather than a static PDF pile, and can't collide with the existing "Intelligence" nav item.
- Risk (ambiguity on first scan) is covered by the homepage section kicker "The Lab · daily" and the hub's dek — the label is never seen without context.
- Rejected: "Briefings" (collides with the firm-leader hero CTA "Book a briefing"), "The Wire" (cute, but reads as a publication brand the site hasn't earned yet), "Resources" (generic; fallback only if Chris wants zero cleverness).

Nav order: **How we help · Intelligence · The Lab · Assessment · Who we are · [Book a private call]** — The Lab sits before Assessment so the free path precedes the ask.

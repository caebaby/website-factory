# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Experienced U.S. financial advisors, typically five to twenty or more years registered with a meaningful book, arriving from cold outbound while privately considering a firm change. They are skeptical of recruiter incentives, sensitive to confidentiality, fluent in industry language, and often researching from a throwaway account because openly asking questions can itself create risk.

## Product Purpose

Advisor Growth Lab turns public movement records from SEC, FINRA, and BrokerCheck data into a private, evidence-based read on an advisor's transition position. The homepage must quickly establish relevance and trust, convert a curious cold visitor into an MQL through the three-minute readiness assessment, and convert a qualified visitor into an SQL through a booked private transition read. Success includes an honest stay, wait, or explore answer, not simply a placement.

## Brand Personality

Calm, forensic, candid. The experience should feel like a discreet pre-dawn research desk: informed, private, and human, with the confidence to say stay when staying is right.

## Anti-references

Recruiting mills, lead-generation funnels, generic fintech navy-and-gold sites, purple-gradient SaaS landing pages, stock-photo handshakes, inflated deal math, fabricated social proof, surveillance language without immediate privacy reassurance, and polished templates that feel machine-generated.

## Design Principles

1. Earn trust before asking for commitment. State the privacy model and conflict disclosure in plain language.
2. Mirror the advisor's private questions immediately. Recognition should happen in the first screen and continue through the page.
3. Show the evidence, not recruiting theater. Use public-record mechanics, sourced figures, and industry vocabulary.
4. Keep the conversion ladder explicit. The assessment is the low-friction MQL; the private read is the SQL.
5. Make honesty visible. Stay, wait, and explore must all feel like legitimate outcomes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain at least 4.5:1 body-text contrast, visible keyboard focus, semantic headings and landmarks, 44-pixel touch targets, reduced-motion fallbacks, no information conveyed by color alone, and responsive layouts without horizontal page scrolling.

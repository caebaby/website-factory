## 01 · HERO

### Swap 1 — Hero subheadline, middle sentence (`#heroSub`)
**SLOT:** `.hero__sub` — second sentence only; first and last sentences unchanged.
**CURRENT:** "If we reached out to you, that's why — the data said the moment was worth your attention."
**PROPOSED:** "If we reached out to you, that's why — something in the public record — your firm, your channel, the movement around you — said the moment was worth a look."
**WHY:** A verify-mode cold arrival discounts "the data said" as recruiter abstraction; naming *which* data (firm, channel, surrounding movement) gives them something checkable, and checkable is the whole brand.

## 02 · RESONANCE MARQUEE (`#resonate`)

### Swap 2 — Section H2
**SLOT:** `.reso__h`
**CURRENT:** "Does this <i>resonate</i> with you?"
**PROPOSED:** "How many of these are <i>yours?</i>"
**WHY:** "Resonate" is consultant vocabulary (kill-list spirit — this crowd talks in grids and bps, not resonance); "how many are yours" presumes plural wounds (usually true) and turns a yes/no into a count they run involuntarily.

**WHY:** Frees "yours" for the H2 where it works harder, and lowers the bar to scrolling — one wound is enough to justify continuing.

## Hero H1 Alternatives (current: "Think clearly about your next move.")

The incumbent is gold-approved and the strategy doc re-ranked it #1; these are challengers for an outbound-skeptic test, not recommendations to swap blind.

1. **"See the whole board before you move."**
   Case: Power Line #4 — names the concrete deliverable instead of a state of mind, which is what a verify-mode arrival can actually evaluate; it's the r/CFP peer-comparison behavior stated as a promise.

2. **"Know where you stand — even if it's 'stay.'"**
   Case: plants the one differentiator no competitor can copy (unmonetized honesty) in the first second — the fastest possible disarm of a cold-email reader's "everyone's paid to move me" filter.

3. **"Decide in private, on evidence."**
   Case: compresses the outbound skeptic's two live anxieties — being seen and being sold — into six words, and pairs cleanly with the "Confidential" chip directly above it.

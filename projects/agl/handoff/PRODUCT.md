# Advisor Growth Lab

## Register

brand

## Users

Experienced financial advisors who are quietly evaluating whether their current firm, business model, economics, growth support, succession options, or transition package still fits the practice they want to build. They need useful answers without being pushed into a recruiting process before they are ready.

## Product Purpose

Advisor Growth Lab is an independent consultancy and editorial resource that helps advisors evaluate transition decisions with evidence. The site should earn trust, capture qualified interest through a short assessment, and convert advisors who are ready into private conversations while giving earlier-stage visitors substantive research and podcast content.

## Brand Personality

Calm, candid, and exact. The experience should be warm and approachable without becoming soft, corporate, theatrical, or overly serious. The writing should sound like an experienced human advisor to advisors, not a recruiter, a generic financial institution, or an AI-generated publication.

## Anti-references

- Generic navy-and-gold financial-services sites.
- AI-generated landing pages with vague claims, repetitive cards, bubbly layouts, or synthetic-sounding copy.
- Aggressive recruiting funnels that treat every visitor as ready to move.
- Overly feminine, clinical, hospital-like, or overly decorative visual treatments.
- Editorial affectations that make the content harder to scan or diminish the conversion path.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the advisor's decision, not the recruiting package.
2. Make evidence, sources, and tradeoffs visible enough to earn trust.
3. Let visitors advance privately and at their own pace.
4. Connect every editorial page to a useful next question and a proportionate next step.
5. Prefer specific, natural language over slogans, jargon, or AI-sounding abstractions.
6. Treat SEO and generative-search discovery as a publishing-system requirement, not a page-level afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for public pages and interactive assessment flows. Preserve semantic HTML, keyboard access, visible focus, reduced-motion support, sufficient contrast, descriptive labels, and readable line lengths.

The next stage starts with the constraint in front of you.

Your selection is for orientation only and is not submitted from this page. We can pressure-test it together on the call.

Genesis → Sustainability

Build a recurring floor beneath the practice.

New revenue is still required to meet the practice and household budgets. Every sale carries weight, and the business can feel as if it resets each month.

Next milestone: recurring revenue covers business and personal expenses.

What we would examine
  • Revenue mix and recurring-income target
  • Repeatable client acquisition and follow-up
  • Planning, service, and marketing systems
Sustainability → Capacity

Give the calendar back to the advisor.

Recurring revenue now covers business and personal expenses. The practice has a floor beneath it, but the advisor may still be carrying too much of the work.

Next milestone: demand can grow without every task and follow-up returning to you.

What we would examine
  • Delegation and workflow design
  • Staffing coverage and role clarity
  • Marketing that does not require constant manual effort
Capacity → Expansion

Turn personal production into an organized team.

The practice has demand and momentum, but time is the binding constraint. The advisor's calendar, attention, and ability to process work are limiting growth.

Next milestone: growth no longer depends entirely on your personal hours.

What we would examine
  • Client segmentation and service model
  • Associate economics, roles, and handoffs
  • Sales execution and team accountability
Expansion → Owner/Operator

Build the company beneath the book.

The advisor is building a company rather than simply enlarging a personal book. Associates, team structure, and multiple lanes of revenue become central.

Next milestone: you can step out of daily delivery without the business losing momentum.

What we would examine
  • Leadership structure and operating cadence
  • Repeatable systems across advisors and teams
  • Management information and decision ownership
Owner/Operator → Exit

Make the enterprise transferable.

Systems, technology, leadership, and clearly owned roles allow the company to run without the founder serving as the sole machine.

Next milestone: relationships, economics, and operations continue without the founder as the machine.

What we would examine
  • Succession and second-generation leadership
  • Recurring economics and practice value
  • Governance, continuity, and transferability
Exit → Optionality

Choose the outcome and protect your terms.

The company functions in the founder's absence. It can acquire, support succession, or be positioned for a financial exit without sacrificing continuity.

Next milestone: a deliberate succession, sale, acquisition, or reduced founder role with client and team continuity.

What we would examine
  • Desired timeline and founder role
  • Successor or buyer readiness
  • Client, team, and ownership continuity

A useful first call, not a recruiting pitch.

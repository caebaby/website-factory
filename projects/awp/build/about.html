The Missing Role

Expert advice can still be fragmented.

Your CPA may know your tax return. Your attorney may know your estate documents. Your investment advisor may know your portfolio. But when each professional sees only one part, the client is left carrying context between rooms.

Anchor exists to own that space between the specialists: maintaining one living plan, putting decisions in sequence, and making sure the right people have the right context before deadlines arrive.

The objective is not more meetings. It is fewer disconnected decisions.

The Anchor Role

Keep every decision on the same course.

  1. 01

    See the whole picture

    Investments, taxes, estate work, equity compensation, and business interests belong in one view.

  2. 02

    Set the sequence

    Each decision has an owner, a dependency, and a deadline.

  3. 03

    Coordinate the room

    Your outside professionals receive the context they need to do their best work.

Origin Story

Why I left the wirehouse.

I spent 10 years inside Merrill Lynch. I learned the business from the inside — the institutional discipline, the resources, the scale. I also learned something that bothered me enough to leave.

The model works for the firm. It doesn't always work for the client.

My clients at Merrill were oil & gas executives, business owners, and families with real complexity. Equity comp. Deferred income. Closely-held businesses. Estate plans that hadn't kept up with their wealth. And in almost every case, the same gap: their CPA, their attorney, and their investment advisor were operating in separate rooms. The client was the one running between them, holding it all together.

I left to close that gap.

Philosophy

How I think about this work.

Wealth management isn't portfolio management. A rebalanced portfolio doesn't fix a tax trap. A model allocation doesn't coordinate with your estate attorney. Real wealth management is the work that happens between the professionals — the tax strategy that informs the investment decision that aligns with the estate plan. That's the work most advisors leave to the client. It's the only work I do.

If your advisor can't sit down with your CPA and have an informed conversation about your deferred comp election, they're not managing your wealth. They're managing your money. There's a difference.

Approach

What makes this different.

I specialize. I don't take clients I can't help — and I tell people that directly. If you're a wage-earner with a 401(k) and no complexity, I'm not the right advisor for you, and I'll refer you to someone who is.

I coordinate. I work alongside your CPA and your estate attorney, not around them. We run joint planning meetings. We maintain one living document. When one professional makes a move, the others know.

I'm independent. I answer to my clients, not to a corporate parent. The advice you get from me is the advice I'd give my own family — because there's no product quota, no proprietary fund, and no conflict between what's best for you and what's best for the firm.

The Problem

You’re holding it all together. That’s the problem.

If your financial life feels like a group project where you’re the only one doing the coordination, you’re not alone. Here’s what we hear — in the words of people who finally found their way here.

No one sees the full picture. My CPA handles taxes, my advisor handles investments, my attorney has the estate docs — and none of them talk to each other. I’m the one playing phone tag between all of them.
01 — Coordination

Your financial life has outgrown the siloed model. You have a CPA, an advisor, and an estate attorney — each excellent at their piece, none aware of the others. You’ve become the project manager of your own wealth, and you’re paying for the gaps in between.

My RSUs vest, I get a huge tax hit, and by the time I think about selling to diversify, I’m emotionally attached to the stock and it’s already dropped.
02 — Equity comp

Equity compensation is how the energy industry pays its leaders. But RSU vesting, deferred comp elections, and blackout periods create a tax-timing problem that generalist advisors don’t understand. Every bonus season feels like a trap because the planning window closes before you act.

We have a trust that was set up years ago. I’m honestly not sure if it’s even funded correctly, or if it still matches what we actually own.
03 — Estate drift

Your net worth has grown. Your estate documents haven’t. Trusts set up at a different stage of life, beneficiary forms that haven’t been updated, and a growing sense that the gap between your wealth and your plan is widening every year.

I know my business inside and out, but I have no idea what it’s actually worth or what I’d walk away with after taxes if I sold.
04 — Illiquid net worth

Your net worth is locked inside an illiquid entity. Your personal finances and business finances are tangled. And if an unsolicited offer arrived tomorrow, you wouldn’t have a team ready — because your advisor has never been through a liquidity event.

How It Works

Here’s what working with us actually looks like.

A 30-minute conversation. No pitch. You tell us about your situation — your equity comp, your business, your family, your concerns. We listen, ask questions, and tell you honestly whether we can help.

Assessment

We review your full financial picture — investments, tax returns, equity documents, estate plan, business interests. We identify the gaps between where you are and where your wealth needs to be. You get a written assessment, whether or not you become a client.

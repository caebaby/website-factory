How RSU vesting creates a tax-timing trap.

Vesting, withholding, blackout periods, and concentration risk rarely arrive on a convenient schedule.

Resources / Article

Educational sample · By Anchor Wealth Planning · [Date]

An RSU vest looks simple on a statement: shares arrive, taxes are withheld, and the remaining position becomes yours. In practice, that single event may touch cash flow, estimated taxes, portfolio concentration, trading restrictions, and long-term planning at the same time.

The trap is the collision of timelines

Your company controls the vesting date. Tax rules control when income is recognized. Trading windows control when shares can be sold. Markets control the price. Your personal plan has to absorb all four.

Planning question: If the stock falls after vesting but before you can sell, where will the tax payment come from?

Default withholding may not equal your final liability

Automatic withholding can create the impression that the tax issue is settled. For higher earners, the amount withheld may differ from the eventual liability. The appropriate response depends on the full tax picture and should be coordinated with a qualified tax professional.

A better decision sequence

  1. Map every expected vest, bonus, deferred compensation election, and trading window.
  2. Estimate the tax impact with the CPA before the event.
  3. Set a concentration policy before emotion or market movement controls the sale decision.
  4. Connect proceeds to the cash-flow, investment, and estate plan.

Questions worth answering early

What percentage of net worth depends on one company or one industry?

Salary, bonus, retirement benefits, and equity may all share the same economic risk.

Which decisions have hard deadlines?

Some elections cannot be changed after the window closes. A coordinated calendar makes those moments visible.

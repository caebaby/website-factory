The Problem

Does this sound familiar?

High-income professionals in Houston face a common problem: financial complexity that no generalist advisor is built to handle.

"My CPA handles taxes. My advisor handles investments. Neither one talks to the other — and I'm the one paying the price."

The siloed advisory problem is the most common issue among executives managing complex accounts, RSUs, deferred compensation, and estate needs across multiple professionals who never coordinate.

"Every bonus season I feel like I'm leaving money on the table. By the time I think about it, the tax window has already closed."

Equity compensation timing — RSUs, deferred comp elections, and bonus planning — requires proactive coordination months in advance, not last-minute decisions on April 14th.

"I've built real wealth, but I don't have a plan for what happens to it after me. My estate documents are from 2014."

Legacy planning is the most commonly deferred item among high-net-worth families. Most discover gaps in their estate plan during a life event — not before.

"I'm making more than I ever have — and somehow I still feel behind. I'm not sure my advisor specializes in situations like mine."

Generalist advisors serve a wide range of clients. Executives and business owners with equity comp, deferred income, and estate complexity often need a specialist who's built for their situation specifically.

The Problem

You're holding it all together. That's the problem.

If your financial life feels like a group project where you're the only one doing the coordination, you're not alone. Here's what we hear — in the words of people who finally found their way here.

01

"No one sees the full picture. My CPA, my advisor, my attorney — and none of them talk to each other."

Your financial life has outgrown the siloed model. You have a CPA, an advisor, and an estate attorney — each excellent at their piece, none aware of the others. You've become the project manager of your own wealth, and you're paying for the gaps in between.

02

"My RSUs vest, I get a huge tax hit, and the stock has already dropped before I diversify."

Equity compensation is how the energy industry pays its leaders. But RSU vesting, deferred comp elections, and blackout periods create a tax-timing problem generalist advisors don't understand. Every bonus season feels like a trap because the planning window closes before you act.

03

"We have a trust. I'm honestly not sure it's funded correctly, or that it still fits what we own."

Your net worth has grown. Your estate documents haven't. Trusts set up at a different stage of life, beneficiary forms that haven't been updated, and a growing sense that the gap between your wealth and your plan is widening every year.

04

"I know my business inside and out — but not what I'd walk away with after taxes if I sold."

Your net worth is locked inside an illiquid entity. Your personal and business finances are tangled. And if an unsolicited offer arrived tomorrow, you wouldn't have a team ready — because your advisor has never been through a liquidity event.

What We Do

One plan. Your CPA and attorney both work from it.

Most advisors manage your investments and stop there. We coordinate your investments, taxes, estate, and business strategy into a single living document — and we make sure your CPA and attorney are reading from the same page.

01

Coordinated Planning

"No one sees the full picture."

We run joint planning meetings with your CPA and estate attorney, and maintain one living document all three professionals reference. When your CPA makes a tax move, your portfolio reflects it. When your attorney updates your trust, your investment strategy adjusts. You stop being the translator.

02

Investment Management

"My advisor doesn't understand my situation."

Built around your actual life — your vesting schedule, your blackout periods, your commodity exposure — not a model portfolio. We manage concentration risk, tax-loss harvesting, and asset location in coordination with your tax strategy, not in isolation.

03

Tax Strategy & Coordination

"Every bonus season feels like a tax trap."

We sit down with your CPA before year-end — not after. We model RSU vesting events, deferred comp elections, and bonus timing against your actual tax situation. Proactive tax planning, not reactive tax preparation.

04

Equity Compensation Planning

"My RSUs feel like a tax trap every year."

RSU vesting schedules, NQDC elections, 10b5-1 plans, blackout-period strategy. We model concentration risk against the commodity cycle and build a diversification plan that respects your constraints — not one that ignores them.

05

Estate & Legacy Planning

"I have real wealth but no plan for what comes after me."

We review your existing trusts against your current net worth, then coordinate with your estate attorney to fund trusts correctly, update beneficiaries, and ensure your documents still match your life. Legacy planning is a living process, not a one-time document.

06

Business Owner Planning

"I don't know what I'd walk away with if I sold."

We start the exit conversation 3–5 years before the LOI. Entity structure optimization, personal and business wealth separation, pre-sale tax strategy, and post-liquidity management. By the time a buyer appears, you're ready.

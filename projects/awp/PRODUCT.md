# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Houston oil and gas executives, closely held business owners, and high-net-worth families whose financial lives span investments, taxes, estate planning, equity compensation, and business interests. They arrive skeptical of sales language and tired of coordinating advisors themselves. Their job is to decide whether Alex understands their situation and whether a first conversation is worth taking.

## Product Purpose

Position Anchor Wealth Planning as the specialist who coordinates a client's CPA, attorney, and investment strategy into one living plan. The site must help the right visitor recognize their exact problem, self-select into one of three client groups, complete the four-question fit assessment, or schedule a 30-minute conversation. Success is qualified MQL and SQL conversion without invented claims or compliance shortcuts.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, assured, and human. Institutional discipline without wirehouse stiffness. Houston-specific fluency without oil-and-gas costume. Maritime language stays sparse and belongs to the established identity, not section-by-section metaphor.

## Anti-references

- Generic navy-and-gold advisor templates with centered stock-photo heroes and vague “holistic wealth” copy.
- Corporate wirehouse sites that feel product-led, distant, or sales-heavy.
- Editorial-magazine affectation: repeated tiny labels, ornamental italics, and ruled grids unrelated to the client journey.
- App-like dashboards, decorative finance charts, and fake planning interfaces.
- Flashy luxury signals, private jets, watches, skyline clichés, or imagery that performs wealth instead of showing real life.

## Design Principles

1. Show the three client worlds immediately, then let visitors choose the one that feels like theirs.
2. Make coordination tangible: every section points back to one living plan shared across professionals.
3. Use calm specificity as proof. Industry language, clear process, and honest `[VERIFY]` markers beat generic authority claims.
4. Treat motion as narrative. Hero footage identifies who Anchor serves; interaction always moves toward the fit assessment or call.
5. Keep Alex human and local. Warm documentary imagery, direct copy, and Houston context should carry more trust than luxury styling.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve keyboard access, visible focus, semantic controls, sufficient text contrast over every video frame, captions or transcripts for future spoken video, reduced-motion fallbacks, poster-first media, and fully usable content without JavaScript.

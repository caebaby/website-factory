# Copy Strategy — Anchor Wealth Planning
Date: 2026-06-26
Strategist: Agent 02

## Positioning

Anchor Wealth Planning is the Houston fiduciary firm founded by an ex-Merrill Lynch advisor that coordinates a client's CPA, estate attorney, and investment strategy into one living plan — built specifically for oil & gas executives, business owners, and high-net-worth families whose financial lives have outgrown generalist advice.

**Against what alternative:** the siloed wirehouse/generalist model where the client is the project manager stitching together CPA, advisor, and attorney.
**Why different:** not just "holistic" (every firm claims that) — the *mechanism* is the differentiator: joint planning meetings with the client's existing professionals, one living document all three reference, and specialist fluency in oil & gas equity comp that no generalist owns.

## Hero Headlines (ranked)

### 1. [RECOMMENDED] — "How do you turn a high income into lasting financial security when no one is looking at the whole picture?"

**Rationale:** This is the intake's hero question, refined. It names the core wound (siloed advice — "no one is looking at the whole picture") inside the desire (lasting security). It's a question the prospect has been unable to answer. It passes the "so what?" test because it implies the problem is structural, not a knowledge gap. Traceable to: ICP research "Exact Words (Pain)" — siloed-advice quotes; Competitive Gap #1.

**Supporting eyebrow:** Houston, TX · Fiduciary Wealth Management

### 2. "Your CPA, your estate attorney, and your advisor shouldn't need you to translate between them."

**Rationale:** Names the coordination pain with maximum specificity. Memorable because it's a sentence the prospect has *thought* but never heard said out loud. Slightly narrower — works as a subhead or a section H2. Traceable to: Power Line #2; Competitive Gap #4.

### 3. "You shouldn't have to be the project manager of your own financial life."

**Rationale:** The most direct articulation of the #1 pain. Bold, punchy. Risk: it's strong as a subhead or pain-section headline but may be too abstract as a hero H1 for a cold visitor who hasn't yet recognized the pain. Strongest as a supporting line. Traceable to: Power Line #1.

## Pain Section

Order and framing for the 4 pain points (Section 3 — THE PROBLEM). Each pain must connect to a service in Section 7.

1. **"No one sees the full picture."** — Lead with this because: it's the universal #1 pain across all three ICPs, and it frames the entire positioning. Your CPA, advisor, and attorney work in silos. You're the one holding it together — and paying for the gaps. *Connects to: Coordinated Planning service.*
2. **"My RSUs and bonus feel like a tax trap every year."** — Oil & gas exec pain. Concentrated equity, blackout periods, deferred comp elections, and a planning window that closes before you act. Generalists don't understand the cycle. *Connects to: Equity Compensation Planning.*
3. **"I have real wealth but no plan for what comes after me."** — HNW family / estate pain. Trusts set up years ago, never revisited. Net worth has tripled; documents haven't. *Connects to: Estate & Legacy Planning.*
4. **"I know my business inside and out, but I have no idea what I'd walk away with if I sold."** — Business owner pain. Net worth locked in an illiquid entity, no exit-readiness path, personal and business finances tangled. *Connects to: Business Owner Planning.*

## Services Framing

Present services as pain-solutions, not a menu. Each service names the client pain it solves first, then describes what AWP does.

1. **Coordinated Planning** — "One plan your CPA and attorney both work from." → Solves Pain #1 (siloed advice). This is the umbrella service — frame it first.
2. **Investment Management** — "Built around your actual life, not a model portfolio." → Connects to concentrated equity and commodity-cycle exposure.
3. **Tax Strategy & Coordination** — "We sit down with your CPA before year-end — not after." → Solves the reactive-vs-proactive tax pain.
4. **Equity Compensation Planning** — "RSU vesting, deferred comp, blackout periods — modeled against your actual schedule." → Solves Pain #2.
5. **Estate & Legacy Planning** — "Trusts that still match your actual net worth." → Solves Pain #3.
6. **Business Owner Planning** — "We start the exit conversation 3–5 years before the LOI." → Solves Pain #4.

**Framing rule:** Never list services as features. Always lead with the pain the prospect recognizes, then show the service as the answer.

## Language Rules

**Always use:**
- "Coordinate" / "coordination" (the mechanism, not the claim)
- "Fiduciary" (stated and defined)
- "Fee-only" (stated plainly)
- Specific industry terms fluently: RSU, deferred comp, blackout period, Permian Basin, commodity cycle, stepped-up basis, trust funding
- "Conversation" (not "consultation") for the first meeting
- First person for Alex: "I," "we," "my clients"
- Direct address: "you," "your CPA," "your estate attorney"

**Never use (from kill list + research):**
- comprehensive, tailored, customized, holistic, unparalleled, cutting-edge
- leverage (as verb), empower, dedicated to, passionate about, committed to
- peace of mind, navigate complexity, client-centric, results-driven
- "partner with you on your journey"
- "In today's complex world..."
- "...so you can focus on what matters most"
- "Whether you're [A] or [B]..."
- "We understand that..."
- Three-adjective clusters
- "decades of combined experience"
- "seasoned professionals"
- Overuse of maritime metaphors (sparingly, per brand guide — "anchor," "charting the course" only)

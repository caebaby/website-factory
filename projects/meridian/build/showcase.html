The Coordination Gap

You hired experts. They just don't talk to each other.

01

Fragmented advice that costs you

Your CPA reduces your taxable income. Your advisor grows your portfolio. Neither knows what the other is doing — and the gaps between them cost you more than either saves you.

02

Your estate plan is a drawer document

You signed the documents. You paid the attorney. But your beneficiaries, titling, and investment accounts still don't match — and no one has reviewed it since you signed.

03

Business and personal wealth in silos

You built a business worth millions. But your personal financial plan treats that business like a line item. The transition plan, the liquidity event, the tax strategy — none of it is coordinated.

04

Complexity that grows faster than your team

At $3M, you could manage it yourself. At $15M, you needed a team. At $30M, you need that team to actually coordinate — and someone whose job it is to make sure they do.

05

Nobody owns the whole picture

You have an advisor, a CPA, an attorney, maybe an insurance agent. Nobody has visibility into everything. Nobody is asking how one decision affects all the others.

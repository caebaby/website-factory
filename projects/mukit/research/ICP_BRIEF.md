# ICP Brief — Mukit Mahdin portfolio
*(Stage 1 · Agent 01 Research. Derived from INTAKE + the reality of how engineering portfolios are read. Exact-words / insider language for the copy agent.)*

## The three readers (a portfolio serves all three in one scroll)

### A. Recruiter / hiring manager (the 8-second scan)
- **Job to be done:** decide in one viewport "is this worth a call?"
- **Exact-words pain:** *"I can't tell if they actually built the thing or just sat near it."*
- **What flips them:** scope + ownership words ("owned end-to-end," "architected," "shipped to production") and **numbers with units in the first screen.**
- **Insider language:** "end-to-end ownership," "cross-functional," "production," "scope," "impact."

### B. Founder / US enterprise client (the buyer)
- **JTBD:** find an AI-first engineer who can translate a business problem into a shipped system without hand-holding.
- **Exact-words pain:** *"I need someone who can own delivery, not just write tickets."*
- **What flips them:** dollar-denominated outcomes ($10K–$20K/month), the client-facing track record (Advisor Growth Lab, US enterprise clients), and a clear "here's how I work."
- **Nervousness:** timezone / "1+ year" seniority optics → override with proof of scope.

### C. Senior engineer (the depth check)
- **JTBD:** verify real depth before spending interview cycles.
- **Exact-words pain:** *"Résumés list frameworks; I want to see systems thinking."*
- **What flips them:** the *mechanism* — factory/resolver AI generation pattern, async pipeline on AWS Lambda with webhook callbacks + vector-DB ingestion, versioned MongoDB-backed prompt management, WAF-bypass scraping with batch checkpointing, weighted-loss class-imbalance handling. Named architecture beats named tools.

## The through-line (the positioning)
**"Ships AI-first systems at scale — with the dollar impact to prove it."**
The number "1+ year" is the objection; **documented business impact + architectural depth** is the answer. Lead with impact, back it with mechanism.

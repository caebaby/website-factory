## Positioning Statement

CPP helps married business owners and executives who already give — to their family, their community, their causes — build a "living legacy": a plan they can watch work while they're alive, run by a fiduciary team structured to outlast any single advisor, without the wealth-signaling or death-coded language the rest of the industry defaults to.

**Why this wins:** it's the one thing not a single competitor in the niche says (COMPETITOR_BRIEF gap #1), it's traceable word-for-word to Abby's own framing, and it pre-answers CPP's biggest structural trust risk (MassMutual/commission-affiliation skepticism, ICP_BRIEF "Fears & Objections") by leading with fiduciary + team continuity instead of hiding it.

---

Every competitor found has at most 1-2 disconnected assets (a podcast alone, a guide alone, a quiz alone). **The white-space gap isn't any single asset — it's the connective tissue.** So the strategy for every asset below answers the same question: *what does this asset do to move someone from not knowing CPP exists → booked conversation?* Nothing is a standalone brochure.

## 2. Assessment (the MQL-capture lead magnet)

**Working name:** "The Living Legacy Snapshot" (or "Legacy Readiness Assessment")

**Why an assessment specifically:** COMPETITOR_BRIEF gap #3 — only one competitor (OneAscent) runs an interactive assessment in this niche, and it's aimed at existing UHNW prospects inside an institutional site, not warm self-identification for a business-owner couple. This format is wide open. It also matches the ICP's "prudent, considered decision-maker" temperament better than a cold call-to-action — they want to think, not be sold.

**Structure (5-7 questions, 3-5 min):**
1. Where are you in the business ownership lifecycle? (building / approaching a sale / post-sale / passing to next gen) — routes them into the right nurture track (this directly maps to the "Current Triggers" in ICP_BRIEF: valuation rise, liquidity event, next-gen entry).
2. How would you describe your approach to charitable giving today? (not yet formalized / informal but consistent / structured giving vehicle) — surfaces the charitable-identity segment competitors ignore.
3. If something happened to you tomorrow, how confident are you that your family/team would know what to do? (continuity question — surfaces the Transfer-of-Trust need without naming it yet)
4. Which feels closer to true: "My plan is mostly about what happens after I'm gone" vs. "My plan is something I actively watch work today"? (this is the diagnostic question — it directly operationalizes the living-legacy vs. estate-planning distinction as a self-assessment, which is genuinely novel in this niche per the competitor scan)
5. Are decisions about money and values made together as a family, or mostly by you alone?
6. (Contact capture) Name / email / phone to see your results.

**Output:** a simple 3-tier result ("Building," "Transitioning," "Living Legacy Ready" or similar) with a short, specific written takeaway per tier — not just a score. Each tier result ends with the Primary CTA to book a conversation, and triggers the matching Email Series track (Asset 5).

**Compliance note:** static, pre-scripted, MassMutual-compliance-cleared copy — no dynamic/generated advice claims, per INTAKE §10.

## 4. VSL / Training Video

**Working title:** "Legacy Planning Without Waiting to Die" or "The Living Legacy Framework" (short training, not a full webinar — matches the "season one, static asset" sequencing decision from the call).

**Why this asset, specifically:** it's the direct parallel to the "productivity course" Chris built in his prior business — a pre-built asset that pre-handles the #1 objection (a topic-specific fear or misconception) so the sales conversation starts pre-sold. Here, the objection to pre-handle is the fiduciary/affiliation trust gap (ICP_BRIEF's highest-stakes backfire risk) plus the death-coded confusion about what "legacy planning" even means.

**Structure (10-15 min, scripted for compliance):**
1. Reframe: legacy-as-active-practice vs. legacy-as-death-event (the Avidian distinction, dramatized with a relatable scenario — e.g., two families, one that waited, one that didn't).
2. The 3 things families with an active living-legacy plan do differently (values conversations, charitable structure, next-gen involvement) — ties to family-governance research.
3. How CPP gets paid, addressed directly on camera (kills the affiliation objection inside the training itself, not just on a separate page).
4. CTA: take the Assessment (if not already done) or book a conversation.

**Distribution:** gated behind the Assessment result page (delivered as the "next step" regardless of tier) and/or as email #2-3 in the nurture sequence.

## 5. Podcast

**Per the call decision:** Season 1 as a static, bounded asset first — not an open-ended weekly commitment — until CPP knows what they can sustain.

**Positioning gap to fill (COMPETITOR_BRIEF #4):** Beacon Family Office's "Legacy Builders" is the best-executed podcast in the niche but skews UHNW/family-office scale with no charitable angle. Legacy Life Financial has the closest tonal match (faith + family + legacy) but thin production and no MQL capture path. **CPP's opening: a mid-market-business-owner-scaled, charitable-giving-forward podcast that actually captures leads** — nobody else in the scan does both.

**Season 1 structure (6-8 episodes, case-study format, not talk-show format):**
- Each episode = one theme from the pain/trigger research: approaching a sale, bringing kids into the business, structuring a charitable vehicle, "what if my advisor leaves," family values conversations that didn't happen and what it cost, etc.
- Guest format optional — Andy/Abby/partners can host solo case-study episodes if guest-booking adds too much logistics overhead (matches their stated capacity constraints from the call).
- Every episode description links to the Assessment, not just a generic "contact us."

**Distribution:** hosted on the website content hub + Spotify/Apple/YouTube (per the original Axiom-playbook principle Chris pitched: be where the traffic already is, then pull back to owned assets).

## 6. Email Series (nurture, MQL → SQL)

**Structure: branch by Assessment result tier**, not one generic sequence — this is the connective-tissue advantage no competitor has (COMPETITOR_BRIEF gap #4).

**All tracks share emails 1-2, then branch:**
1. **Immediate:** Assessment result delivery + the VSL/training video as "the next 12 minutes worth your time."
2. **Day 2-3:** A story-video email — "the family we built this for" — pairs warmth + competence per the ICP_BRIEF pattern, reinforces they're not a number.
3. **Branch — "Building" tier:** slower-cadence, education-forward (podcast episodes on values conversations, charitable structuring) — this segment isn't in a hurry, so don't rush the CTA.
4. **Branch — "Transitioning" tier (approaching sale/liquidity event):** faster cadence, direct to the fee-transparency/fiduciary page and a specific "18 months before you sell" urgency point pulled straight from ICP_BRIEF triggers.
5. **Branch — "Living Legacy Ready" tier:** shortest sequence, fastest to the direct booking CTA — they're already closest to conversion, don't over-nurture.
6. **All tracks converge:** a "here's exactly how we're paid" email mid-sequence (never later than email 4) — the affiliation objection has to be pre-handled before the ask, not left for the sales call.
7. **Final email in every track:** direct, specific booking CTA — "a 20-minute conversation, no pitch, just a look at where you stand."

**Kill-list applies to subject lines and body copy identically** — no "trusted advisor," "comprehensive," etc.

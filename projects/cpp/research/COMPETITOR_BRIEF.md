## Competitive Gaps (White Space CPP Can Own)

1. **"Living legacy" is claimed in name only, never in mechanism.** Every firm above that uses "legacy" in its headline — OneAscent, Faithward, Mission Wealth, Alterra — still resolves the concept into inheritance, wealth transfer, estate documents, or "after death" language somewhere on the page. Alterra comes closest with an explicit estate-vs-legacy distinction, but even they frame it through a succession-planning/technical lens, not a real-time, "decisions you make while you're alive together as a family" lens. **No one in this scan has built an entire site architecture, quiz, or content system around legacy as a present-tense, real-time practice rather than a future-tense transfer event.** This is CPP's single clearest and most defensible gap — it's the literal words Abby used on the intake call, and no competitor owns them.

2. **Charitable giving as core identity, not an add-on.** Every values/faith-based competitor treats philanthropy as a service line (impact investing, donor-advised funds, "giving" as a menu item). None center it as a *defining trait of the ICP* the way CPP's intake does ("ideally have a charitable arm... legacy to us means real world, real time"). CPP can lead with "we work with families who already give — this is how we help you give and build wealth without choosing between them," which no competitor page currently says.

3. **Assessment/quiz format is underused and, where used, poorly targeted.** Only OneAscent runs an interactive assessment (Values-Alignment Assessment), and it's aimed at existing UHNW prospects inside an institutional-feeling site, not warm self-identification for a business-owner couple. No competitor uses a quiz as a top-of-funnel MQL-capture tool the way CPP's asset slate plans to. This format is wide open in the niche.

4. **Podcast + VSL + quiz + email nurture as one integrated funnel doesn't exist anywhere in this scan.** Beacon (podcast only), Values Added (video + e-course only), Mission Wealth (guides + app only), Alterra (guide + newsletter only) each have one or two assets — none combine podcast, assessment, VSL/training video, and email sequence into a single MQL→SQL system. CPP's planned asset stack, if built and connected, would be structurally unique in the niche, not just better-produced.

5. **Anti-wealth-signaling visual/brand tone is a documented but unfilled need.** Firms like Faithward and Mission Wealth still lean on stock-photo affluence cues (multi-office skylines, generic "wealthy family" imagery). None explicitly reject status-signaling imagery the way CPP's intake does (yacht/luxury car rejected as "dumb"/inauthentic). A warm, tactile, non-ostentatious visual language paired with "living legacy" copy would look distinct against every competitor above.

6. **Business-owner-specific "Transfer of Trust" / team-first framing is absent.** Every competitor's site centers 1-2 named founder-advisors as the brand (Phil Shaffer at OneAscent, named advisors at Faithward, etc.). None frame the *firm* as the client relationship with advisors as interchangeable trusted partners — CPP's equity-model / "partners not junior planners" structure is a genuine structural differentiator no competitor site messages.

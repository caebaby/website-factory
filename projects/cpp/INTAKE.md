## 3. Who They Serve (ICP) — in Andy's own words

> "We are looking for business owners and executives who are part of a family, who are married, ideally have a charitable arm, and are interested in legacy. And legacy to us means real world, real time, not death. They want to make prudent decisions with their money when they are interested in legacy." — Abby Spaulding, verbatim from the call

Breakdown:
- **Who:** business owners and executives
- **Family status:** married, family-oriented (not single/childless-focused)
- **Values:** charitable giving as a core identity marker, not an afterthought
- **Core motivator:** "legacy" — reframed explicitly as *living* legacy (decisions made now, while alive), not estate/death planning
- **Behavior:** prudent, considered decision-makers — not chasing yield or performance bragging rights

**What have they tried before that didn't work:** Two prior website iterations that missed the mark — one "too simple," one that leaned into wealth-signaling imagery (yacht, luxury car) that felt inauthentic to the brand. They know what they don't want more clearly than what they do want.

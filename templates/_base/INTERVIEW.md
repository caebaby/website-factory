# The Founder Interview — 60 minutes that outperform 60 hours of web research

**What this is:** the question set Chris (or any operator) runs with a higher-tier client before
the factory prints their site. Record it, transcribe it (Fathom/Whisper), save the transcript to
`projects/<client>/research/INTERVIEW_TRANSCRIPT.md`. It becomes a first-class source: the
research chain treats it as the highest authority on *voice* and *objections*, and the VOC
LEDGER cites it by name.

**Why it works:** web research finds what prospects say in public. The founder has heard what
they say in private — on sales calls, at the point of almost-buying, in the words they used when
they finally said yes. That language converts. No amount of Reddit mining recovers it.

**How to run it:** conversational, not a survey. Ask the question, then chase the STORY — "tell
me about the last time that happened" beats any opinion. Get them telling specific stories about
specific people (anonymized) and the hour will fill itself. The follow-up prompt under each
question is the real question.

---

## Block 1 — The buyer they know best (10 min)
1. Think of your favorite client — the one where, if you had 20 more like them, the business is
   made. Walk me through how they first showed up. *What was going on in their life that week?*
2. What did they say on the first call? *As close to their actual words as you can get — what
   sentence do you still remember?*
3. What had they already tried before you? *Why didn't those work — in THEIR telling, not yours?*
4. When did you know they were going to sign? *What changed in the conversation?*

## Block 2 — The pain behind the pain (10 min)
5. When a prospect describes their problem, what do they SAY it is — and what is it actually?
   *Where's the gap between the presenting problem and the real one?*
6. What does this problem cost them that they never say out loud? *(Sleep, marriage strain,
   feeling behind their peers, embarrassment — the felt cost.)*
7. What finally makes them move after months or years of tolerating it? *What's the trigger
   event you see over and over?*

## Block 3 — Objections, verbatim (10 min)
8. What's the #1 thing that almost stops a good-fit prospect from signing? *What are the exact
   words they use when they raise it?*
9. What's the objection they DON'T voice — the one you have to answer before they'll ask it?
10. Tell me about a deal you lost that you should have won. *What did they say when they told
    you no? Who did they choose instead, and why?*
11. What do prospects believe about your category that's flat wrong? *What happens when you
    correct it — relief, resistance, or disbelief?*

## Block 4 — The alternative and the enemy (8 min)
12. When they don't hire you, what do they do instead? *(DIY, a cheaper competitor, nothing —
    which is the real competition?)*
13. What do your competitors all say that makes prospects' eyes glaze? *What's the phrase this
    market is completely numb to?*
14. What's the thing you do that competitors genuinely can't or won't? *How would a CLIENT
    describe that difference — not how would you?*

## Block 5 — The moment it worked (8 min)
15. Tell me about a client's "it's working" moment — when they realized hiring you was right.
    *What did they say or send you? (Emails and texts here are proof-inventory gold.)*
16. If your best client described you to a friend at dinner, what would they actually say?
    *Not your positioning — their sentence.*
17. What result are you proudest of that you can PROVE? *What's on paper? (This feeds the proof
    inventory — and what can't be proven gets [VERIFY]'d, never fabricated.)*

## Block 6 — Voice and limits (8 min)
18. Read them 3–4 candidate phrases/headlines from early research (if available). *Which would
    you actually say to a prospect's face? Which made you wince? Why?*
19. What words does this market use that outsiders get wrong? *What marks someone as "not one
    of us"?*
20. What can we legally/ethically NOT say? *(Compliance regime, forbidden claims, guarantees —
    the sacred limits. For advisors: SEC Marketing Rule vs FINRA 2210 matters here.)*
21. What do you NEVER want your brand to sound like? *Name a competitor site that repels you.*

## Block 7 — Close (5 min)
22. What question did I not ask that I should have?
23. If this site does its job perfectly, what does the person reading it DO — and what state of
    mind are they in when they do it?

## After the interview — routing the transcript
1. Save transcript → `research/INTERVIEW_TRANSCRIPT.md` (raw, unedited; timestamps fine).
2. Run the ICP Deep Research Pack with the transcript listed in the intake as a primary source —
   stage 02 (VOC extraction) mines it alongside web sources.
3. Agent 01 then cites it in the VOC LEDGER as `source: interview` rows — the highest-authority
   voice evidence in the system.
4. Anything the founder claimed as fact (numbers, results, credentials) that isn't independently
   verifiable goes to the `[VERIFY]` register, not into copy. Their language is gospel; their
   stats are leads to verify. (Axiom rule: never fabricate, never inflate.)

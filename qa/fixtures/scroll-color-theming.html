Scroll to recolor the page

The page is the canvas, not the frame.

As you scroll, the whole ground shifts color and the type inverts to stay legible. Each section declares a paired theme; the background crossfades between them.

Dark · #0E0E0C
data-bg #0E0E0C / data-fg #FAFAF8

Smooth, not abrupt

A 0.7s crossfade reads as intentional, not a flicker.

The transition is a single CSS animation on a fixed layer, driven by an IntersectionObserver at the section midpoint. No scroll-jank, no per-frame work.

Light · #FAFAF8
data-bg #FAFAF8 / data-fg #14140F

The accent swaps too

On a jewel ground the accent shifts to stay visible.

Each theme carries its own accent so a CTA never disappears against the new background. The pairing of background, foreground, and accent is the contract.

Jewel · #103B2E
data-bg #103B2E / data-fg #EAF3EE / accent #E7A23C

Use sparingly

Three to five theme moves is a signature. Recoloring every section is noise.

Without JS or under reduced-motion, every section above is still a solid, readable, correctly-colored block. The crossfade is the enhancement, never the load-bearing floor.

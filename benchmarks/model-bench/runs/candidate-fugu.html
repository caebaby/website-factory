The decision

Recruiting calls create noise. Moving firms creates risk.

You are being told the grass is greener by people paid to say it. The hard part is not finding someone who wants your book. It is knowing whether a move improves your life, your economics, and your clients’ experience after the excitement wears off.

“Before you take another recruiter call, know what your current position is actually worth.”

AGL operating principle

The pitches blur together

Wirehouse breakaway, independent broker-dealer, RIA platform, tuck-in, succession partner — every option is sold as freedom. The terms hide the tradeoffs.

The numbers are hard to trust

Deal grids, payout math, transition notes, custodian costs, staffing, compliance burden — one missed assumption can make a good-looking move fragile.

Timing matters

Advisors tend to move around recognizable career, firm, and market signals. If you only look when a recruiter calls, you are already late to the analysis.

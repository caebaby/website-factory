Your practice deserves more than a comp comparison.

A transition changes more than payout. It affects client experience, control, technology, staffing, succession, growth capacity, and the eventual value of the business. Private research puts those variables in one decision frame.

01

Business-model alignment

Which operating model best supports the clients, services, team, and degree of control you want?

02

Portability and transition risk

What portion of the book is relationship-driven, what constraints apply, and what could interrupt client continuity?

03

Economics beyond the headline

How do payout, expenses, forgivable notes, transition costs, growth assumptions, and time horizon change the real result?

04

Practice value and exit options

Does the model help you create a more transferable enterprise, and does it support the exit you eventually want?

How the work comes together.

The public record establishes the market context. Your private answers determine whether that context is relevant.

01 / PUBLIC RECORD

Movement, registration, and firm context.

SEC, FINRA, BrokerCheck, public filings, and cited industry research.

02 / PRIVATE CONTEXT

Your book, economics, team, and goals.

The facts that cannot be inferred from a registration record.

03 / DECISION FRAME

Stay, wait, or explore a transition.

A practical next step, including the possibility that no move is warranted.

What this is not

It is not a valuation, legal opinion, or recruiting offer.

The research is designed to improve the questions and comparisons you bring into a private conversation. Legal, tax, valuation, and employment questions should be reviewed with the appropriate professionals.
